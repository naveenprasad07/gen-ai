**Motivation Title:** "Empowering India: A Vision for a Developed Nation"

**Introduction:** 
Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's departing speech emphasizes the importance of youth, rural development, and partnerships in achieving India's development goals. He shares 10 important messages gained during his interactions with people from different walks of life, highlighting the potential for transformation in India.

**Summary of the Speech in 10 Key Points:**

1. **Accelerate Development by Fulfilling Aspirations of Youth**: India's 540 million youth are the driving force behind the country's development.

2. **Empower Villages**: Rural empowerment is essential for bridging the rural-urban divide and creating opportunities for growth and development.

3. **Mobilize Rural Core Competence for Competitiveness**: Empowering villages can lead to the development of sustainable economic growth in rural areas.

4. **Focus on Agricultural Growth from Seed to Food**: The country needs to focus on agricultural growth, from the seed to the food, to increase agricultural GDP growth.

5. **Overcome Problems through Determination and Success**: Determination and success are key to overcoming obstacles and challenges.

6. **Overcome Problems through Partnerships**: Partnerships between government, private sector, and civil society organizations are essential for overcoming problems and achieving success.

7. **Show Courage in the Face of Calamities**: India needs to show courage in the face of calamities, such as natural disasters, to overcome them.

8. **Foster Connectivity for Societal Transformation**: Connectivity is essential for societal transformation, and it can be achieved through various means, including physical, electronic, and knowledge connectivity.

9. **Defend the Nation and Take Pride in its Security**: National security is essential for the country's development, and it requires the bravery and dedication of its defense forces.

10. **Lead a Youth Movement to Achieve a Developed India by 2020**: Empowering the youth is crucial for achieving a developed India by 2020, and it requires a value-based education and leadership.

**Key Messages:**

* Empowering villages and connecting them to urban societies is essential for India's development.
* Partnerships between government, private sector, and civil society organizations are crucial for achieving success.
* Determination and success are key to overcoming obstacles and challenges.
* National security is essential for the country's development.
* Empowering the youth is crucial for achieving a developed India by 2020.

**Conclusion:** 
Dr. A. P. J. Abdul Kalam's speech emphasizes the importance of empowering villages, connecting them to urban societies, and empowering the youth to achieve a developed India by 2020. He shares his mission in life, which is to bring people together in India's multicultural society, fostering self-confidence and a sense of "we can do it."
