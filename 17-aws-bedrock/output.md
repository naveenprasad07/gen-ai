**A Canine Portrait in Shakespeare’s Muse**

When sunrise paints the morn with golden hue,  
A modest beast doth stir beneath the dew;  
His coat, a tapestry of russet, gold,  
Like autumn’s fire that doth the forest hold.  

His eyes, twin orbs of sable, bright and keen,  
Do mirror loyalty, pure, unseen;  
With every bark—a trumpet bold and clear—  
He calls the world to hear his hearty cheer.  

His limbs, like supple arches tipped with spring,  
Do bound across the fields where robins sing;  
A nose, most keen, doth scent the hidden trail,  
And finds the crumbs where hidden treasures hail.  

Yet, ’midst his strength, a gentle soul resides,  
A faithful heart that in his master hides;  
Thus, humble hound, though lowly in his sway,  
Doth wear the crown of love each dawning day.  

— In iambic grace, this verse doth end,  
For dogs, the truest friends, our hearts commend. 

**A Sonnet to the Luna’s Pale Embrace**

O gentle orb that rides the night’s soft tide,  
Thy argent face doth pierce the sable dome;  
In silent grace thou wander’st far and wide,  
A shepherd of the stars, yet tempest‑free home.  

When winter’s chill hath frozen mortal breath,  
Thy silver beams do melt the frosted veil;  
The lovers’ hearts, by whispered sighs, draw near,  
For in thy glow they find a love‑filled tale.  

Yet oft thou wanes, a shy and waning maid,  
Behind the clouds thy shy visage doth hide;  
But even then, the heavens mark thy trade,  
And bloom the night with promise yet untried.  

So hail, O Moon, in thy eternal glow,  
The night’s own crown, where humble mortals bow.

**Upon the Vastèd Stage of Heaven’s Dome**  

O wond’rous vault, where endless night doth gleam,  
A furnace bright, where suns like gilded dreams  
Do blaze in solemn court of boundless space;  
Each star a sovereign, each a fated grace.  

The firmament—‘twixt darkness and the light—  
Doth spin in perfect rhythm, soft and spite,  
Like argent wheels that turn the mighty round,  
Whilst countless worlds in silent veils are bound.  

From nebular mist, the infant realms arise,  
In swaddling clouds of dust, ‘midst sighs and sighs;  
The planets waltz, and comets swift do race,  
As zephyrs whisper truth of endless space.  

O Time, thou art the loom that weaves the span,  
The tapestry of ages, man and man;  
Yet even thou, with all thy measured tread,  
Can scarce behold this ever‑growing spread.  

Thus, look thou up, dear mortal, and be still—  
For spangled heavens hold the boundless will,  
A theater vast where all of being plays,  
And Shakespeare’s quill could scarce its wonders phrase.
**A Mother’s Praise, in Shakespearean Strain**  

O gentle rose that blossoms ’midst life’s storm,  
Her tender hand the cradle of our breath;  
In tender shade she shields from fate’s foul norm,  
And weaves with love a mantle free of death.  

She bears the weight of suns that daily rise,  
Yet soft as silken night when stars convene;  
Her voice, a lullaby that never lies,  
Doth calm the restless heart, serene, unseen.  

When sorrow’s tempest rages fierce and grim,  
She is the harbor where our hopes abide;  
A steadfast rock, her spirit never dim,  
In every trial, the faithful guide.  

Thus, let the heavens sing, and mortals vie,  
For none can match a mother’s boundless sky.
